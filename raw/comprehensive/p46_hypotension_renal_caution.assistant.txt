A colleague suggests increasing carvedilol and enalapril because the patient has HFrEF. Is that safe with this BP and creatinine trend?

smart_toy

Not safe to up‑titrate right now. With SBP 86 mmHg plus cool peripheries and rising creatinine, treat this as possible cardiogenic shock / poor renal perfusion (HF/shock is listed as a cause of acute renal failure) [UCG 7.1.1]. Both ACE inhibitors and beta blockers require caution when SBP <90 mmHg and renal function should be monitored [UCG 4.1.3]. The GDMT titration tables also require SBP >90 mmHg for enalapril up‑titration and carvedilol up‑titration, and advise holding ACEi if creatinine rises >30% (and holding ACEi/MRA if K ≥5.5) [GDMT Tables]. So increasing carvedilol/enalapril in this scenario is not appropriate.

Single next step: get immediate senior/physician review and urgent referral/transfer arrangements (this is a red flag: hypotension with poor perfusion and AKI trend) [UCG 7.1.1]. Any action to hold, reduce, or increase enalapril/carvedilol/spironolactone/diuretics is a medication change and requires cardiologist/physician confirmation before administration.

To decide what to tell the doctor and what to prioritize during referral, what are today’s creatinine value (and baseline), potassium, and current urine output, and is she currently congested (basal crepitations/JVP/leg oedema) or more “dry”?
