Medication classes — how to explain them to Joseph (and what to monitor)

Clinically, in HF we use a loop diuretic when hypervolemic; HF Algorithm lists furosemide PO 20–40 mg OD/BID with a typical daily range up to 160 mg/day (IV use in acute settings) [HF Algorithm]. ACE inhibitor (enalapril): “This opens blood vessels and reduces salt/water retention, making it easier for the heart to pump.” Watch for symptoms of low BP; ACE‑Is can cause a dry cough [PocketDoktor]. Beta blocker (carvedilol): “This blocks stress hormones (adrenaline) so the heart doesn’t have to work as hard or beat as fast.” Do not stop suddenly unless told by a doctor; watch for low BP/slow pulse symptoms [PocketDoktor].
