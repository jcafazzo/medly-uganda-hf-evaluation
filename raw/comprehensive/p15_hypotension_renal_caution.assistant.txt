A colleague suggests increasing carvedilol and enalapril because the patient has HFrEF. Is that safe with this BP and creatinine trend?

smart_toy

With SBP 82 mmHg + dizziness + cool peripheries + rising creatinine, it is not safe to up‑titrate enalapril or carvedilol now. Both UCG and the GDMT tables require SBP >90 mmHg for ACE inhibitor and carvedilol titration, and UCG specifically cautions ACE inhibitors and beta blockers when SBP <90 mmHg with renal function monitoring [UCG 4.1.3; GDMT Tables]. The HF Algorithm also flags cardiogenic shock (hypotension, poor perfusion) as a red flag requiring urgent attention/escalation [HF Algorithm].

Single next step (do now): treat this as possible shock/poor perfusion and immediately escalate to the most senior clinician/medical officer and arrange urgent transfer to hospital (and communicate “suspected cardiogenic shock/poor perfusion in HFrEF with AKI”) [HF Algorithm].

Any change to HF medicines (holding, reducing, increasing, switching, or giving extra diuretic/vasodilator) requires cardiologist confirmation before administration—if there is no onsite cardiologist, contact the on‑call/referral cardiologist/physician urgently before making the change.

To guide what the referral clinician will most urgently need (and to differentiate cardiogenic shock vs hypovolaemia/other causes), what are his current SpO₂, heart rate, urine output, and is he volume overloaded (JVP raised, basal crepitations, oedema) or dry on exam? [HF Algorithm; UCG 4.1.3]
