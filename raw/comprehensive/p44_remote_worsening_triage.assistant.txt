Single next step (remote): arrange immediate referral/transport now and keep her upright while this is being organised. In acute pulmonary oedema, the guideline immediate action is to prop the patient up in sitting position and give high-concentration oxygen aiming SpO₂ >95% once she reaches care [UCG 4.1.4]. If she has a pulse oximeter and reports SpO₂ <90%, that is hypoxaemia and a life-threatening emergency [UCG 1.4].

Escalate as an emergency now if she has severe dyspnoea, cough with frothy blood-stained sputum, or features of acute pulmonary oedema [UCG 4.1.4].
Escalate as an emergency now if SpO₂ <90%, or if she has danger signs such as inability to talk in full sentences, extreme weakness, confusion/sleepiness/agitation even without an SpO₂ reading [UCG 1.4].
