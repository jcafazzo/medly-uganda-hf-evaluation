With SBP 89 mmHg + dizziness and cool peripheries plus a creatinine rise, it is not safe to up‑titrate carvedilol or enalapril right now—this fits a low-perfusion state/cardiogenic shock physiology, and UCG explicitly cautions ACE inhibitors and beta blockers when SBP <90 mmHg and to monitor renal function [UCG 4.1.3]. Single next step: Treat this as an unstable patient and escalate urgently for physician/cardiology review and hospital-level care (and manage as possible shock/AKI while arranging transfer), rather than making outpatient HF dose increases [UCG 7.1.1; HF Algorithm]. Any medication change (including increasing, holding, or reducing enalapril/carvedilol/spironolactone/diuretics) requires cardiologist confirmation before administration.

Key safety issues to flag before any HF medicine changes (for the receiving clinician/cardiologist):

Hypotension with symptoms (SBP <90): ACEi/beta blocker use requires caution; avoid uptitration in this state [UCG 4.1.3; GDMT Tables].
Worsening renal function: ACEi titration guidance includes hold if creatinine rises >30% and monitor Cr/K after changes [GDMT Tables]. AKI can be due to heart failure/shock (compromised renal perfusion) and needs urgent evaluation/monitoring and referral if worsening [UCG 7.1.1].
Beta blocker titration: do not uptitrate carvedilol if decompensated/volume overloaded; requires SBP >90 and HR >55 [GDMT Tables].
MRA (spironolactone): needs K/renal monitoring; hold if K ≥5.5 [GDMT Tables].
