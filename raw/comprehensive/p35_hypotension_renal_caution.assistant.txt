A colleague suggests increasing carvedilol and enalapril because the patient has HFrEF. Is that safe with this BP and creatinine trend?

smart_toy

With SBP 82/48, dizziness, cool peripheries, and rising creatinine, up‑titrating enalapril or carvedilol is not safe right now. Both UCG and the GDMT tables note SBP >90 mmHg as a requirement for ACE‑I/beta‑blocker titration, and UCG explicitly cautions ACE‑I and beta blockers when SBP <90 mmHg with renal-function monitoring [UCG 4.1.3], [GDMT Tables]. The GDMT tables also advise holding enalapril if creatinine rises >30% or if there is symptomatic hypotension [GDMT Tables]. The HF Algorithm flags hypotension/poor perfusion as a red flag requiring urgent attention [HF Algorithm].

Single next step now: treat this as suspected shock/poor perfusion and urgently escalate for senior doctor review and hospital-level care/transfer. While arranging this, UCG shock guidance supports keeping him lying down with raised legs and rapid assessment of perfusion/response at the bedside [UCG 1.1.2].

Any change to his HF medicines (including holding enalapril, carvedilol, spironolactone, or changing diuretic dosing) requires cardiologist confirmation before administration.

To decide whether this is primarily cardiogenic shock vs hypovolaemia/over-diuresis vs sepsis/other trigger, what is his heart rate and SpO₂ right now, and does he currently have signs of congestion (basal creps/raised JVP/leg oedema) or is he clinically “dry”?
