face

A colleague suggests increasing carvedilol and enalapril because the patient has HFrEF. Is that safe with this BP and creatinine trend?

smart_toy

With SBP 86 mmHg + dizziness + cool peripheries + rising creatinine, this is not a situation to up‑titrate carvedilol or enalapril. Both UCG and the titration tables emphasize caution/holding escalation when SBP is <90 or there is symptomatic hypotension and worsening renal function: UCG says use ACE inhibitors and beta blockers with caution if SBP <90 and monitor renal function [UCG 4.1.3], and the GDMT tables specify hemodynamic requirement SBP >90 mmHg for enalapril and carvedilol and to hold enalapril if creatinine rises >30% or symptomatic hypotension [GDMT Tables].

Single next step (before changing any HF medicines): Treat as possible cardiogenic shock/poor perfusion and escalate immediately to a senior clinician/receiving hospital for urgent review and referral (this is a red-flag state in the HF Algorithm) [HF Algorithm]. Any holding, reducing, or increasing enalapril/carvedilol/spironolactone/furosemide is a medication change and requires cardiologist confirmation before administration/withholding—even if the “cardiologist” is reached by phone via the referral center.

To support that escalation call and decide what the cardiologist will likely advise, what are her current HR, SpO₂, RR, mental status, and urine output (and what is the current creatinine value and baseline value so we can estimate the % rise)? [GDMT Tables; UCG 4.1.3]
