2) What each HF medicine is for (and what to watch for)

Use plain language like: “These medicines either help remove extra fluid, or reduce the strain on the heart so it can pump better.”

Furosemide (Lasix) – the ‘water pill’: helps kidneys remove extra water and salt, reducing leg swelling and breathlessness. Tell her to expect follow-up blood tests sometimes (kidney function/electrolytes). [PocketDoktor]
ACE inhibitor (enalapril): helps open blood vessels and reduces salt/water retention, making it easier for the heart to pump. Watch for low blood pressure symptoms (dizziness) and sometimes dry cough. [PocketDoktor]
Beta blocker (carvedilol): blocks adrenaline so the heart doesn’t have to work as hard or beat as fast; do not stop suddenly unless a doctor tells her to. Watch for dizziness or a very slow pulse. [PocketDoktor]
Spironolactone: helps block stress hormones that stiffen/scar the heart; she should expect blood tests to monitor kidney function and potassium. [PocketDoktor]
