A colleague suggests increasing carvedilol and enalapril because the patient has HFrEF. Is that safe with this BP and creatinine trend?

smart_toy

With SBP 89 mmHg + dizziness + cool peripheries + rising creatinine, this is not a safe moment to up‑titrate enalapril or carvedilol. Both UCG and the GDMT tables require SBP >90 mmHg for ACE‑i and carvedilol titration, and UCG explicitly says to use ACE inhibitors and beta blockers with caution if SBP <90 mmHg and to monitor renal function [UCG 4.1.3; GDMT Tables]. The GDMT tables also advise holding ACE‑i if creatinine rises >30% from baseline or if symptomatic hypotension [GDMT Tables].

Medication safety issue to flag: up‑titrating now risks worsening hypotension/perfusion and kidney injury. Any dose increase (or holding) is a medication change and requires cardiologist confirmation before administration.

Single next step now (before changing HF medicines): Treat this as possible shock/poor perfusion and escalate immediately to senior clinician/receiving hospital for urgent review/transfer while you reassess perfusion and oxygenation. (HF Algorithm lists cardiogenic shock/red flags as requiring urgent attention) [HF Algorithm].

These determine whether this is primarily cardiogenic shock/decompensation (and how urgently to transfer) and whether ACE‑i meets the “Cr ↑ >30%” hold threshold [GDMT Tables; HF Algorithm].
