A colleague suggests increasing carvedilol and enalapril because the patient has HFrEF. Is that safe with this BP and creatinine trend?

smart_toy

With SBP 89 mmHg + dizziness + cool peripheries + rising creatinine, it is not safe to up‑titrate carvedilol or enalapril right now. Both UCG and the GDMT titration tables require SBP >90 mmHg for ACE‑I and beta blocker titration, and UCG specifically cautions ACE‑I/beta blockers when SBP <90 mmHg with renal function monitoring; GDMT tables also flag holding ACE‑I if creatinine rises >30% from baseline or if symptomatic hypotension. [UCG 4.1.3], [GDMT Tables]

Because this involves potential medication dose changes/holding, any medication change requires cardiologist confirmation before administration (if no onsite cardiologist, call the on‑call/receiving cardiologist/medical officer urgently).

Single next step (before any med changes): treat this as possible cardiogenic shock/poor perfusion with AKI and escalate immediately to physician/senior review and arrange urgent transfer while you reassess perfusion and oxygenation. (Cool peripheries + hypotension + renal deterioration are red flags; the HF Algorithm lists cardiogenic shock as a red flag requiring urgent attention.) [HF Algorithm], [UCG 7.1.1]

One key question to decide how urgent/what to communicate to the receiving team: What is his current SpO₂, heart rate/rhythm (ECG if available), urine output, and exact creatinine change (baseline → now, and potassium if you have it)?
