Once you confirm she is stable, I will help you explain (in patient-friendly terms) the purpose and key warnings for:

Furosemide (“water pill”)—removes excess water/salt; expect monitoring blood tests [PocketDoktor].
ACE inhibitor (enalapril)—opens blood vessels and reduces salt/water retention; watch for low BP and possible dry cough [PocketDoktor].
Beta blocker (carvedilol)—blocks adrenaline so the heart beats slower and with less effort; do not stop suddenly; watch for low BP/slow pulse [PocketDoktor].
Spironolactone—blocks stress hormones that stiffen/scar the heart; expect kidney function/potassium blood tests [PocketDoktor].

And the most important lifestyle points to tell her:

Low-salt, low-fat diet; avoid adding salt and packaged foods [PocketDoktor]; salt/fluid restriction are also part of chronic HF care [UCG 4.1.3].
Fluids: discuss an individualized limit; in severe HF she may be advised <1.5 L/day [PocketDoktor] (UCG suggests 1–1.5 L/day in HF care) [UCG 4.1.3].
Alcohol: avoid/limit alcohol [PocketDoktor], [HF Algorithm].
Activity: regular exercise within symptom limits [UCG 4.1.3]; avoid overexertion [PocketDoktor].
Home monitoring: daily weight if possible; seek care for rapid weight gain (≥1 kg overnight or 2–3 kg/week) and for resting breathlessness or worsening swelling [PocketDoktor].
