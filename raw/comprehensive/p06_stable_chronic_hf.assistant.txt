What to tell Sarah (patient-friendly, aligned to Uganda guidance)

1) Salt and fluids

Salt: “Salt makes the body hold onto water, which can worsen swelling and breathing.” Advise reduce salt intake (avoid adding salt and very salty/packaged foods) [UCG 4.1.3] [PocketDoktor].
Fluids: “Too much fluid can overload the heart.” Uganda guidance recommends limiting fluids to about 1–1.5 L/day (tailor to clinician plan, especially if swelling/breathlessness) [UCG 4.1.3] [PocketDoktor].

2) Alcohol and smoking

“Alcohol can worsen heart failure; try to minimize or avoid it.” [HF Algorithm] [PocketDoktor]. (Smoking avoidance is also encouraged as part of living well) [PocketDoktor].

3) Activity

Encourage regular exercise within her symptom limits: “Keep active, but don’t overexert—stop and rest if you are very short of breath or dizzy.” [UCG 4.1.3] [PocketDoktor].

4) What each medicine is for (simple explanations)

Furosemide (Lasix): “A water pill that helps the kidneys remove extra salt and water—this reduces leg swelling and fluid in the lungs.” Patients should expect periodic blood tests for electrolytes/kidney function [PocketDoktor]. (Clinically, HF Algorithm uses loop diuretics when hypervolemic) [HF Algorithm].
Enalapril (ACE inhibitor): “Opens blood vessels and helps the body hold less salt and water—makes it easier for the heart to pump.” Watch for symptoms of low BP; ACE inhibitors can cause a dry cough [PocketDoktor]. UCG notes ACE inhibitors are part of chronic HF therapy, increased gradually if tolerated [UCG 4.1.3].
Carvedilol (beta blocker): “Blocks stress hormones so the heart doesn’t have to work as hard or beat as fast.” Important: do not stop suddenly unless a doctor tells you [PocketDoktor]. (HF Algorithm targets HR 60–80 when titrated) [HF Algorithm].
Spironolactone: “Blocks hormones that cause the heart to become stiff and scarred.” Expect kidney function and potassium monitoring [PocketDoktor]. (It is listed as an additional medicine for chronic HF in UCG and as an MRA in the HF Algorithm) [UCG 4.1.3] [HF Algorithm].

5) Home monitoring & when to seek care

Ask her to monitor breathing, ability to exercise, and leg swelling day to day [PocketDoktor].
If she has access to weight checks: “Seek care if weight increases ≥1 kg overnight or 2–3 kg in a week” [PocketDoktor].
Teach warning symptoms requiring urgent review: severe shortness of breath while seated/resting, severe dizziness/fainting, rapidly worsening swelling, or severe chest pain [PocketDoktor].

Important safety note (meds): Any starting, stopping, or dose adjustment of HF medicines (including “extra” furosemide) requires cardiologist confirmation before administration.
