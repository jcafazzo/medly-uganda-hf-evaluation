face

A colleague suggests increasing carvedilol and enalapril because the patient has HFrEF. Is that safe with this BP and creatinine trend?

smart_toy

With SBP 88 mmHg + dizziness + cool peripheries and a creatinine rise, it is not safe to up‑titrate enalapril or carvedilol now. Both UCG and the GDMT tables specify SBP >90 mmHg as a minimum hemodynamic requirement for ACE‑I up‑titration, and UCG explicitly cautions ACE inhibitors and beta blockers when SBP <90 mmHg with renal function monitoring; the GDMT tables also say to hold ACE‑I if creatinine rises >30% and to avoid beta‑blocker uptitration if volume overloaded/decompensated [UCG 4.1.3], [GDMT Tables].
Any adjustment (increase, decrease, or holding) is a medication change—this change requires cardiologist confirmation before administration.

Single next step: treat this as a red-flag decompensation/possible shock physiology and urgently escalate for senior clinician review and referral/transfer to hospital level while continuing close monitoring [HF Algorithm].

Focused question (to guide what the cardiologist/receiving team will decide about holding vs continuing meds): what is his current SpO₂ and does he have signs of congestion right now (basal crepitations, raised JVP, worsening oedema/weight gain), or is he more “dry” (no oedema, low JVP, poor skin turgor)?
