No—up‑titrating enalapril or carvedilol is not safe right now in a patient with SBP 86 mmHg + dizziness + cool peripheries and a rising creatinine. This constellation suggests cardiogenic shock/poor perfusion, and both UCG and the GDMT tables caution against ACE inhibitor/beta blocker use when SBP is <90 mmHg and/or when renal function is worsening. Specifically, UCG cautions using ACE inhibitors and beta blockers with caution if SBP <90 mmHg and to monitor renal function [UCG 4.1.3], and the GDMT tables give hemodynamic “go/no‑go” thresholds and hold criteria (ACEi requires SBP >90; hold enalapril if Cr rises >30% from baseline; carvedilol requires SBP >90 for titration) [GDMT Tables]. Also, beta blockers should be titrated only when the patient is not decompensated/volume overloaded [HF Algorithm], [GDMT Tables].

Single next step (before any HF medicine changes):
Treat this as shock/critical perfusion and escalate immediately for physician-led care/referral while starting urgent stabilization as per shock management (secure IV access, close monitoring) [UCG 1.1.2].
