A colleague suggests increasing carvedilol and enalapril because the patient has HFrEF. Is that safe with this BP and creatinine trend?

smart_toy

No—up‑titrating carvedilol or enalapril is not safe with SBP 82/48 plus dizziness/cool peripheries and rising creatinine. Both UCG and the GDMT tables set SBP >90 mmHg as a safety requirement for ACE‑I and carvedilol titration, and UCG explicitly cautions ACE‑I and beta blockers when SBP <90 mmHg and to monitor renal function [UCG 4.1.3; GDMT Tables]. The GDMT tables also advise holding ACE‑I if creatinine rises >30% from baseline [GDMT Tables]. In the HF Algorithm, cardiogenic shock is a red-flag state requiring urgent attention, not outpatient titration [HF Algorithm].

Single next step (now): treat this as suspected cardiogenic shock/poor perfusion and urgently escalate to hospital/physician-level care while monitoring closely. This should happen before making any HF medication changes [HF Algorithm].

Medication safety issues to highlight before any changes (for discussion with the receiving physician/cardiologist):

ACE‑I (enalapril): avoid up‑titration with SBP <90 and consider holding if Cr ↑ >30%; monitor K/Cr closely [GDMT Tables; UCG 4.1.3].
Beta blocker (carvedilol): do not up‑titrate unless hemodynamically stable (SBP >90) and not decompensated/volume overloaded [GDMT Tables; UCG 4.1.3].
MRA (spironolactone): needs K/Cr monitoring; hold if K ≥5.5 and only use if eGFR >30, K <5.0 [GDMT Tables].
Diuretics (furosemide): if she is oliguric/AKI, UCG notes IV furosemide may be used “according to response,” but this must be in a monitored setting given shock/hypoperfusion risk [UCG 7.1.1].

Critical safety rule: Any holding, decreasing, or increasing of enalapril/carvedilol/spironolactone/furosemide is a medication change and requires cardiologist confirmation before administration.

To triage immediate stabilization vs shock mimics and to brief the receiving team: what is her current heart rate and rhythm (regular vs irregular/ECG), SpO₂, and urine output (mL/hr or last void)?
