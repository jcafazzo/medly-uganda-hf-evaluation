While you measure those, here is the patient-facing summary you can use now:

Medicines (what they do, in simple terms)
Furosemide (“water pill”) helps the kidneys remove extra salt and water, reducing swelling and breathlessness [PocketDoktor]. In the UCG, furosemide is used to control congestion, with doses increased according to response [UCG 4.1.3].
Enalapril (ACE inhibitor) opens blood vessels and helps the body hold less salt and water, making it easier for the heart to pump [PocketDoktor]. UCG notes to start low and increase gradually if tolerated, and to be cautious if systolic BP is <90 mmHg with renal monitoring [UCG 4.1.3].
Carvedilol (beta blocker) blocks adrenaline so the heart doesn’t have to work as hard or beat as fast; it should not be stopped suddenly unless a doctor advises [PocketDoktor]. UCG adds it when the patient is stable, increasing gradually [UCG 4.1.3].
Spironolactone blocks stress hormones that stiffen and scar the heart; patients should expect periodic blood tests for kidney function and potassium [PocketDoktor]. UCG lists spironolactone as an additional medicine option in chronic HF [UCG 4.1.3].
Lifestyle changes that matter most
Salt restriction + fluid limitation: UCG recommends reduce salt intake and limit fluids to ~1–1.5 L/day in HF (when advised) [UCG 4.1.3]. Explain: “Salt and too much fluid make the body hold water, which worsens swelling and breathing.”
Alcohol: limit or avoid alcohol—PocketDoktor recommends avoiding alcohol as it can worsen HF [PocketDoktor].
Activity/exercise: do regular exercise within symptom limits (don’t overexert) [UCG 4.1.3]; [PocketDoktor].
Adherence: HF needs continuous treatment; staying on medicines and follow-ups helps prevent progression/complications [UCG 4.1.3]; also check for “failure to take meds” as a common trigger [HF Algorithm].
Home monitoring / when to seek care (traffic-light)

Use PocketDoktor’s framework:

Seek help urgently if he has shortness of breath while seated/resting, rapid weight gain, fainting, worsening swelling, or severe chest pain/dizziness [PocketDoktor].
See a clinician if he gains ≥1 kg overnight or 2–3 kg in a week, or symptoms are continuing/worsening [PocketDoktor].
Encourage daily (or frequent) tracking of breathing, exercise tolerance, and leg swelling [PocketDoktor].
