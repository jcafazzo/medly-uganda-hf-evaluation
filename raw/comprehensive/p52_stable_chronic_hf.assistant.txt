While you’re checking that, here is a patient-friendly explanation of the key medicines and lifestyle points you can give him (education only—any medication change requires cardiologist confirmation before administration):

Medicines (what they do, what to watch for)
Furosemide (“water tablet”, e.g., Lasix): helps the kidneys remove extra salt and water, so swelling and breathlessness improve [PocketDoktor]. Tell him to report worsening swelling, sudden weight gain, or breathing trouble, and that blood tests may be needed to monitor salts and kidney function [PocketDoktor].
Enalapril (ACE inhibitor): helps open blood vessels and reduces salt/water retention, making it easier for the heart to pump [PocketDoktor]. Warn about symptoms of low blood pressure (dizziness) and that ACE inhibitors can cause a dry cough [PocketDoktor].
Carvedilol (beta blocker): blocks adrenaline so the heart doesn’t have to work as hard or beat as fast; it helps long-term heart function [PocketDoktor]. Key message: do not stop suddenly unless a doctor tells him, because the heart may race [PocketDoktor]. Also watch for low BP/slow pulse symptoms [PocketDoktor].
Spironolactone (MRA, e.g., Aldactone): blocks stress hormones that cause the heart to become stiff/scarred; helps protect the heart over time [PocketDoktor]. Explain that he should expect ongoing blood tests for kidney function and potassium [PocketDoktor].
(If you are reinforcing the clinical plan) In HFrEF (EF <40%), the HF Algorithm’s core long-term medicines are: loop diuretic if congested, plus ACEi/ARB/ARNi, beta blocker, spironolactone, and SGLT2 inhibitor (if available) [HF Algorithm]. (Starting/adding/titrating any of these still needs clinician/cardiologist authorization.)
Lifestyle that matters most
Salt: advise a low-salt diet—avoid adding salt during cooking and avoid packaged/processed foods [PocketDoktor; UCG 4.1.3].
Fluids: some patients with worse HF are advised to limit fluids; UCG suggests 1–1.5 L/day as a typical restriction in HF management when indicated [UCG 4.1.3]. Encourage him to follow the amount agreed with the clinic [PocketDoktor].
Alcohol & smoking: advise avoid alcohol and stop tobacco—both can worsen heart failure [PocketDoktor].
Activity: encourage regular exercise within his symptom limits (do not overexert), and discuss a safe plan with the clinician [PocketDoktor; UCG 4.1.3].
Daily monitoring: if he has access to a scale, weigh in the morning; seek care if ≥1 kg overnight or 2–3 kg in a week, or if swelling/breathing worsens [PocketDoktor]. PocketDoktor also uses a simple “green/yellow/red” symptom approach: seek help urgently for severe breathlessness at rest, fainting, or rapidly worsening symptoms [PocketDoktor].
