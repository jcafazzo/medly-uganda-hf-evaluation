A colleague suggests increasing carvedilol and enalapril because the patient has HFrEF. Is that safe with this BP and creatinine trend?

smart_toy

With SBP 88/56 + dizziness + cool peripheries + rising creatinine, this is not a situation to up‑titrate carvedilol or enalapril—it suggests poor perfusion/cardiogenic shock or severe decompensation with AKI risk, and UCG explicitly cautions ACE inhibitors and beta blockers when SBP <90 mmHg with renal monitoring needed [UCG 4.1.3]. The GDMT titration tables also require SBP >90 mmHg for ACEi uptitration and carvedilol titration, and advise holding ACEi if creatinine rises >30% or if symptomatic hypotension [GDMT Tables]. Any med increase here could worsen hypotension and renal function.

Medication safety issues to flag (before any HF medicine changes):

ACE inhibitor (enalapril): Use with caution when SBP <90 and monitor renal function; for titration, SBP should be >90 and hold if symptomatic hypotension or Cr rises significantly [UCG 4.1.3; GDMT Tables].
Beta blocker (carvedilol): UCG says add/titrate beta blocker when patient is stable; GDMT tables require SBP >90 and HR >55, and do not uptitrate if decompensated/volume overloaded [UCG 4.1.3; GDMT Tables].
Spironolactone: Needs close K/Cr monitoring; GDMT tables specify K <5.0, eGFR >30 to use safely and to hold if K ≥5.5 [GDMT Tables].
Renal impairment context: UCG renal section advises extra caution with medicines in reduced urine output/renal impairment and to avoid nephrotoxins (e.g., NSAIDs) [UCG 7.1.3].

Escalation: This clinical picture is a red flag requiring urgent senior/doctor review and likely transfer to higher level monitored care (possible cardiogenic shock/AKI). Any medication change (increase, hold, reduce, or extra dose) requires cardiologist confirmation before administration.

Single next step (right now): confirm the severity of shock/instability with a focused reassessment to guide immediate escalation—what is his current HR, SpO₂, mental status (alert/confused), and urine output in the last 6 hours? [UCG 4.1.3]
