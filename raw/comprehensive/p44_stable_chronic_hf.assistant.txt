Medicines (explain in simple terms)

1) Furosemide (Lasix) — “water pill”

“This helps your kidneys remove extra salt and water, so swelling reduces and breathing becomes easier.” [PocketDoktor]
“You may need periodic blood tests because this medicine can affect salts in the blood and kidney function.” [PocketDoktor]
(Clinically: loop diuretic is used when hypervolemic, and dose is adjusted to response [HF Algorithm]. Any change requires cardiologist/doctor confirmation before administration.)

2) Enalapril (ACE inhibitor) — helps the heart pump with less resistance

“It opens blood vessels and helps your body hold onto less salt and water, so the heart works less hard.” [PocketDoktor]
“Watch for symptoms of low blood pressure (dizziness/weakness). It can also cause a dry cough in some people.” [PocketDoktor]
(Clinically: ACEi is part of chronic HF therapy and is started low then increased gradually if tolerated [UCG 4.1.3]; ACEi/ARB/ARNi are core therapy in HFrEF/HFmrEF in the algorithm [HF Algorithm]. Any dose change requires cardiologist/doctor confirmation before administration.)

3) Carvedilol (beta blocker) — protects the heart from stress hormones

“It blocks adrenaline so your heart does not beat too fast and does not work too hard.” [PocketDoktor]
“It can cause low pulse/low BP symptoms; and do not stop it suddenly unless the doctor tells you—your heart may race.” [PocketDoktor]
(Clinically: beta blocker is used when the patient is stable/euvolemic and titrated toward a goal HR 60–80 bpm [HF Algorithm]. UCG also notes to add carvedilol when stable and increase gradually [UCG 4.1.3]. Any dose change requires cardiologist/doctor confirmation before administration.)

4) Spironolactone — blocks harmful stress hormones

“It blocks stress hormones that make the heart stiff and cause scarring.” [PocketDoktor]
“You should expect regular blood tests to monitor kidney function and potassium.” [PocketDoktor]
(Clinically: spironolactone is an add-on HF medicine in the algorithm [HF Algorithm]. Any dose change requires cardiologist/doctor confirmation before administration.)

Fluids
