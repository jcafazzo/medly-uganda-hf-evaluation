face

A colleague suggests increasing carvedilol and enalapril because the patient has HFrEF. Is that safe with this BP and creatinine trend?

smart_toy

With SBP 84/50 and cool peripheries plus a rising creatinine, it is not safe to up‑titrate enalapril or carvedilol right now. Both the UCG and GDMT titration tables indicate these drugs require SBP >90 mmHg and should be used with caution/held when there is symptomatic hypotension or significant worsening renal function—and beta blockers should not be up‑titrated when the patient is decompensated/volume overloaded [UCG 4.1.3], [GDMT Tables]. The HF Algorithm also frames beta blockers as for when the patient is euvolemic (not acutely decompensated) [HF Algorithm].

Medication safety issues to flag before any HF med changes (key points):

Enalapril (ACEi): UCG cautions ACE inhibitors if SBP <90 and to monitor renal function [UCG 4.1.3]. GDMT tables: for enalapril, SBP >90 required, and hold if symptomatic hypotension or if creatinine rises >30% from baseline; also hold if K ≥5.5 [GDMT Tables].
Carvedilol (beta blocker): GDMT tables: SBP >90 and HR >55 required for up‑titration; do not up‑titrate if volume overloaded/decompensated [GDMT Tables]. UCG also cautions beta blockers when SBP <90 [UCG 4.1.3].
Spironolactone (MRA): requires close renal/K monitoring; GDMT tables recommend eGFR >30 and K <5.0 to use, and hold if K ≥5.5 with scheduled K/Cr monitoring (3 days, 1 week, then monthly ×3) [GDMT Tables].
Furosemide: in renal failure/oliguria, UCG allows IV furosemide according to response (high doses may be necessary), but this should be under hospital-level management and monitoring [UCG 7.1.1].

Escalation (before changing HF medicines): Given hypotension + poor perfusion, treat as an emergency and escalate urgently to senior clinician/cardiology/physician-led care and arrange transfer. Any decision to hold, reduce, increase, or give extra doses of enalapril/carvedilol/spironolactone/furosemide is a medication change and requires cardiologist confirmation before administration.

Single next step now: quantify how severe the renal decline is to apply the GDMT “hold” threshold—please calculate/confirm: What is the baseline creatinine, the current creatinine, and the current potassium (K)? [GDMT Tables]
