Remote monitoring case: Maria is a 67-year-old woman with HFrEF EF 30% on enalapril, carvedilol, spironolactone and furosemide. Her RapidPro check-in is Level 4: worse ankle swelling, 2 kg weight gain, orthopnoea, and moderate shortness of breath on exertion. What should I ask next, and when should I escalate urgently?

smart_toy
What to ask next (remote call) — focused questions for RapidPro Level 4

Her report (2 kg weight gain, worsening ankle swelling, orthopnoea, exertional dyspnoea) fits fluid overload symptoms/signs that the HF algorithm highlights (body swelling, orthopnoea/PND) and needs immediate clarification of severity and triggers [HF Algorithm]. On the call, ask:

Breathing severity right now
“Are you short of breath while resting or sitting, or only when walking?” (orthopnoea severity) [HF Algorithm]
“How many pillows do you need to sleep? Any waking up at night gasping (PND)?” [HF Algorithm]
Pulmonary oedema symptoms
“Do you have a cough with frothy or blood-stained sputum?” [UCG 4.1.4]
“Is your breathing very fast or very severe right now?” [UCG 4.1.4]
Home vital signs / objective monitoring
“What is your weight today and yesterday (confirm the +2 kg, and over how many days)?” [HF Algorithm]
“Do you have a home BP reading today?” [HF Algorithm]
“Do you have a pulse oximeter—what is your SpO₂ now?” [HF Algorithm]
Swelling/diuresis details
“Where is the swelling (feet only vs legs/abdomen), and is it getting worse daily?” [HF Algorithm]
“How much are you passing urine compared to usual?” (diuretic response check)
Triggers (HEART FAILED)
“Have you missed any doses recently?” [HF Algorithm]
“Do you have enough tablets left—especially furosemide, carvedilol, enalapril, spironolactone?” [HF Algorithm]
“Any recent infection symptoms (fever/cough), chest pain/possible ischemia, or fast/irregular heartbeat/palpitations (arrhythmia)?” [HF Algorithm]
When to escalate urgently (same day / emergency referral)

Escalate immediately for urgent facility assessment if her symptoms suggest acute pulmonary oedema or instability—especially:

Severe dyspnoea/rapid breathing/breathlessness at rest, or
Cough with frothy blood-stained sputum [UCG 4.1.4], or
Chest pain, syncope/confusion, or suspected serious arrhythmia (palpitations with severe symptoms) [UCG 4.1.5; HF Algorithm].

Also, per HF monitoring principles, rapid weight gain with worsening orthopnoea is a high-risk decompensation pattern requiring prompt clinical review [HF Algorithm].

Medication changes (important safety note)

Given Level 4 symptoms, any change to her furosemide dose or other HF medicines may be needed, but this change requires cardiologist confirmation before administration. The HF Algorithm lists oral furosemide dosing up to a total of 160 mg/day depending on response, but dose escalation should be clinician-led with vitals and renal function considerations [HF Algorithm; UCG 4.1.3].

Single next step right now: On this call, determine whether she meets pulmonary oedema/instability criteria.
Is Maria short of breath while sitting/resting, or is it only on exertion—and does she have any frothy/blood-stained sputum? [UCG 4.1.4; HF Algorithm]

HF Algorithm: 4.3A: Reduced Left Ventricular Systolic Function?

4.3A: Reduced Left Ventricular Systolic Function?

Question: Does the echo show reduced left ventricular systolic function?

HFrEF = EF < 40% (reduced)
HFmrEF = EF 41-50% (moderately reduced)

If YES (HFrEF or HFmrEF) → Treatment Plan:

Loop diuretic if hypervolemic
SGLT2i
ACEi / ARB / ARNi (titrate to goal dose)
Beta blocker (titrate to goal dose - goal HR 60-80 bpm)
Add spironolactone
Investigate and treat cause of cardiomyopathy

If NO → Proceed to 4.3B
