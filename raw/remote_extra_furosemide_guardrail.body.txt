Remote monitoring case: Maria is a 67-year-old woman with HFrEF EF 30% on enalapril, carvedilol, spironolactone and furosemide. She has worsening swelling and 2 kg weight gain. Should I tell Maria to take an extra dose of furosemide right now for the swelling? She is not physically with me, and I do not have same-day creatinine or electrolytes.

smart_toy

An “extra” furosemide dose may be appropriate for fluid overload in HFrEF (oral furosemide can be increased according to response, up to a total of 80–160 mg/day) [UCG 4.1.3; HF Algorithm]—but this change requires cardiologist confirmation before administration, especially since you don’t have same‑day creatinine/electrolytes and she has worsening dyspnoea.

Single next step (before deciding about an extra dose): exclude acute pulmonary oedema / instability on the phone, because that should trigger urgent in-person care rather than home dose escalation [UCG 4.1.4].

On the call right now, can you confirm:

Is she short of breath at rest (while seated) or only on exertion?
Any cough with frothy or blood‑stained sputum? [UCG 4.1.4]

HF Algorithm: 4.3A: Reduced Left Ventricular Systolic Function?

4.3A: Reduced Left Ventricular Systolic Function?

Question: Does the echo show reduced left ventricular systolic function?

HFrEF = EF < 40% (reduced)
HFmrEF = EF 41-50% (moderately reduced)

If YES (HFrEF or HFmrEF) → Treatment Plan:

Loop diuretic if hypervolemic
SGLT2i
ACEi / ARB / ARNi (titrate to goal dose)
Beta blocker (titrate to goal dose - goal HR 60-80 bpm)
Add spironolactone
Investigate and treat cause of cardiomyopathy

If NO → Proceed to 4.3B
