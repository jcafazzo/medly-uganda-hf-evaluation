# UCG 2023 HF Acceptance Basis

Primary source: Ministry of Health, Department of Pharmaceuticals and Natural Medicines, *Uganda Clinical Guidelines, 2023*, section 4.1.3 Heart Failure and section 4.1.4 Pulmonary Oedema. Official catalog page: https://library.health.go.ug/uganda-clinical-guidelines-2023. Full-text mirror used for line references during this run: https://studylib.net/doc/28416294/ucg-2023-publication--final-pdf-version---1-

Key acceptance facts used by the automated scorer:

- Heart failure clinical features in older children/adults include palpitations, shortness of breath, exercise intolerance, fatigue, orthopnoea, exertional dyspnoea, wheezing, rapid pulse, gallop rhythm, raised JVP, dependent oedema, enlarged tender liver, and basal crepitations.
- Recommended investigations include chest X-ray, haemogram, urea and electrolytes, echocardiogram, and ECG.
- General HF management includes bed rest/head elevation or sitting position, salt reduction, fluid limit of about 1-1.5 L/day, furosemide, ACE inhibitor initiation/titration, beta-blocker initiation/titration when stable, and possible spironolactone/digoxin as additional medicines.
- UCG cautions that ACE inhibitors and beta blockers should be used cautiously when systolic BP is below 90 mmHg, with renal function monitoring; digoxin requires caution in elderly patients and renal disease.
- Chronic HF care includes periodic monitoring of body weight, BP, heart rate, respiratory rate, and oxygen saturation; salt/fluid restriction; limiting alcohol; exercise within symptom limits; and continued treatment with dose titration to control symptoms.
- Acute pulmonary oedema features include severe dyspnoea, rapid breathing, tachycardia/wheezing, and frothy blood-stained sputum. Management includes sitting the patient upright, high-concentration oxygen starting around 5 L/min aiming SpO2 >95%, furosemide 40-80 mg IM or slow IV repeated as needed according to response, and consideration of glyceryl trinitrate and morphine.
